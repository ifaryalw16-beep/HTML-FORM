About IIU Islamabad

The International Islamic University Islamabad (IIUI) is a federal chartered university established in 1980. It is a unique institution that blends modern academic disciplines with Islamic values and principles. Located in the heart of Islamabad, IIU is one of Pakistan's most prestigious universities, attracting students from over 50 countries worldwide.

The university offers programs in Islamic Studies, Engineering, Management Sciences, Law, Social Sciences, Medicine, and Arabic Language — all guided by the vision of producing graduates who are academically excellent and morally upright.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is there a quota for foreign students?
Yes. IIU reserves a special quota for international students. Foreign applicants must submit their equivalence certificate from the Inter Board Committee of Chairmen (IBCC) along with their application.
Can I apply for more than one program?
Yes, you may apply for up to two programs simultaneously. However, a separate application form and fee must be submitted for each program.
Is there a Hafiz-e-Quran concession?
Yes. Students who are Hafiz-e-Quran are awarded 20 additional marks in the merit calculation. A valid certificate from a recognized institution is required.
What is the medium of instruction?
The medium of instruction at IIU is English for most programs. However, programs in Islamic Studies and Arabic Language are conducted in Arabic and Urdu.
Are scholarships available for new students?
Yes. IIU offers need-based and merit-based scholarships. Students may apply through the Directorate of Student Affairs after enrollment. HEC scholarships are also available.
